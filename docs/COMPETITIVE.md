# Cognify — Competitive Landscape

> Condensed from the April 9, 2026 research brief (`CogR.pdf` in source materials). Full stats and citations there.

## One-line positioning vs everyone

**"Other tools are Grammarly for speech. Cognify is Duolingo for communication."**

Every funded competitor analyzes what you've said, polishes how you sound, or simulates a specific conversation. None of them train the underlying skill of thinking clearly under pressure. Cognify is the only daily-habit, rep-based, framework-driven training system.

## The competitors

### Yoodli — the $300M+ elephant

- **Stage**: $40M Series B (Dec 2025), $60M total, $300M+ valuation, ~40 employees
- **Positioning**: Enterprise AI roleplay / "experiential learning" platform
- **Customers**: Google, Snowflake, Databricks, RingCentral, Sandler Sales
- **Founders**: Varun Puri (ex-Google X), Esha Joshi (ex-Apple)
- **What they do**: AI personas simulate conversations (sales, coaching, interviews, feedback). Custom scenarios. Instant delivery feedback (filler words, pacing). Web-only (deliberately no mobile).
- **Proof points**: Snowflake saved ~1,215 hrs / $170K per quarter in manager coaching. Harness cut sales training review time 75%.
- **Where they're weak** (Cognify exploit):
  - Enterprise-first pricing, limited free tier, no consumer daily-habit product
  - Scenario-specific roleplay, not foundational skill-building
  - Web-only, no mobile
  - "Practice for this call" not "build the skill"

### Hyperbound — sales roleplay pure-play

- **Stage**: $15M Series A (Sep 2025), $18.3M total, YC
- **Positioning**: AI sales roleplay only
- **Customers**: IBM, LinkedIn, Monday.com, Bloomberg, Vanta, Autodesk, G2
- **What they do**: Turn ICP descriptions into AI buyer personas. Cold calls, discovery, demos, renewals. Multi-party roleplays. Claims 50% ramp time reduction.
- **Where they're weak**: 100% sales-specific. Zero surface for meetings, presentations, interviews, or feedback conversations.

### Speeko — the closest consumer comp (watch carefully)

- **Stage**: Undisclosed funding. 500K+ users.
- **Positioning**: "A gym for your voice" — alarmingly close to Cognify's "gym for communication"
- **Platforms**: iOS, macOS, web. No Android. Built on Google Gemini/Vertex.
- **Pricing**: $24.99/mo or $99.99/yr. Live 1-on-1 coaching from $150/session. Speeko for Business (enterprise pivot underway).
- **What they do**: Record speaking → feedback on pace, tone, filler words, clarity. Courses on storytelling, impromptu speaking, managing nerves. Integrates with Zoom, Slack, Teams, Meet. Content from Roger Love (celebrity voice coach).
- **Where they're weak**:
  - Trains *how you sound* (pacing, fillers, tone), not *how you think* (structure, clarity, organization)
  - No structure training
  - No scenario training with back-and-forth
  - No framework generation
  - Reliability issues (crashes, freezes per app-store reviews)
  - No Android
- **How Cognify differentiates verbally**: Speeko = gym for your voice. Cognify = gym for your thinking.

### Orai — speech analysis

- Small funding (est. <$5M)
- Record → feedback → fix loop on fillers, pacing, tone
- Speaking habits only, not skill building
- No meaningful threat; included for completeness

### SecondNature — AI sales training

- ~$30M+ raised
- AI roleplay for sales conversations, objection handling
- Scripted and narrow
- Not a direct competitor to Cognify's general-communication play

### Mindtickle — courses + coaching

- Content-first learning platform
- Not habit-based, knowledge-first
- Cognify's daily-rep model is structurally different

## Cognify's true differentiators (the unique claims)

1. **Daily practice system** — nobody else has built a structured, daily workout for communication. Duolingo model.
2. **Trains thinking, not delivery** — every competitor focuses on how you sound. Cognify trains how you think.
3. **Consumer-first, habit-first** — every funded player is enterprise. The daily-habit consumer play is unclaimed. Cognify goes dual-track, with consumer as the habit engine feeding the institutional channel.
4. **Framework generation, not scripts** — scenario mode returns a *framework for thinking*, not a conversation to memorize.
5. **Structural-adherence scoring** — a net-new scoring dimension that directly measures whether the user held the framework. No competitor has this because no competitor generates frameworks.
6. **External-validation flagship** — blind listener ranking as a user-facing feature. No competitor offers this because it would expose the weakness of their scoring models.
