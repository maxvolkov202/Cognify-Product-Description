# Cognify Strict Grading System

## Philosophy

**Be analytical and conservative in scoring.**
**Do not inflate scores.**
**Assume the speaker is developing unless proven otherwise.**

## Score Distribution Guidelines

| Range   | Classification | Description                                    |
|---------|---------------|------------------------------------------------|
| 90-100  | Exceptional   | Executive-level performance                     |
| 75-89   | Strong        | Minor refinements needed                        |
| 60-74   | Competent     | Noticeable structural and clarity gaps          |
| 40-59   | Developing    | Missing key components or lacking specificity   |
| <40     | Unclear       | Significant delivery and structural issues      |

**If the response lacks measurable outcomes, strong structure, or clear reasoning, it should not exceed 70.**

## Evaluation Dimensions

### 1. Clarity (20% weight)
**Question:** Is the core idea immediately understandable?

**Penalties:**
- Main point buried under preamble: -15 pts
- Sentences > 25 words average: -15 pts
- Excessive repetition: -10 pts
- Too compressed (< 50 words): -12 pts

### 2. Structure (25% weight)
**Question:** Does the response follow a logical, complete progression and adhere to the selected framework?

**Penalties:**
- Weak opening: -10 pts
- Missing actionable closing: -15 pts
- Underdeveloped (< 3 sentences): -15 pts

**Caps:**
- Missing framework component (when framework selected): Max 60

### 3. Specificity (25% weight) — HARSHEST GRADER
**Question:** Are claims supported with concrete details or measurable outcomes?

**Starting Score:** 55 (must prove specificity)

**Bonuses:**
- Has metrics/numbers: +20 pts
- Has concrete example: +10 pts
- Has specific nouns: +8 pts
- Has causal reasoning: +7 pts

**Penalties:**
- Vague claims without metrics: -20 pts
- Business jargon: -12 pts

**Caps:**
- No metrics AND no concrete examples: Max 60
- Vague claims without evidence: Max 65

### 4. Pacing (15% weight)
**Question:** Was time managed effectively and prioritized correctly?

**Penalties:**
- < 50 words (severely underdeveloped): -25 pts
- < 80 words (rushed): -10 pts
- > 200 words (over-explained): -20 pts
- > 160 words: -10 pts
- Wasted opening on preamble: -12 pts

**Caps:**
- Running out of time or rushing (< 60 or > 180 words): Max 70

### 5. Presence (15% weight)
**Question:** Does the speaker sound confident, steady, and decisive?

**Penalties:**
- 10+ filler words: Cap at 65
- 6-9 filler words: -18 pts
- 3-5 filler words: -10 pts
- 4+ hedging phrases: Cap at 60
- 2-3 hedging phrases: -15 pts
- Confidence issues ("does that make sense?"): -12 pts

**Bonuses:**
- Zero filler words: +12 pts

## Overall Score Calculation

```
overallScore = 
  clarity × 0.20 +
  structure × 0.25 +
  specificity × 0.25 +
  pacing × 0.15 +
  presence × 0.15
```

## Primary Focus Selection

The system identifies ONE primary focus for the next rep based on the lowest **weighted impact** (score × weight).

Priority order:
1. Specificity (25% weight) - "Add Measurable Outcomes"
2. Structure (25% weight) - "Build Complete Structure"  
3. Clarity (20% weight) - "Lead With Your Point"
4. Presence (15% weight) - "Eliminate Filler Language"
5. Pacing (15% weight) - "Manage Time Effectively"

## Diagnostic Messages

### Overall Diagnostics
- **90-100:** "Exceptional executive-level performance."
- **75-89:** "Strong performance with minor refinements needed."
- **60-74:** "Competent but noticeable structural and clarity gaps."
- **40-59:** "Developing. Missing key components or lacking specificity."
- **<40:** "Unclear message with significant delivery and structural issues."

### Tone Guidelines
- Professional, not motivational
- Direct, not praise-heavy
- Analytical, not encouraging
- Performance-based, not supportive

## What This System Fixes

❌ **Before:** Inflated scoring, generic praise, shallow feedback, lack of credibility  
✅ **After:** Honest assessment, specific diagnostics, actionable guidance, professional standards

## Examples of Strict Grading

### Example 1: Vague Answer (Score: ~55)
**Response:** "We improved our process and made things better for customers. It was a good project."

**Grading:**
- Clarity: 60 (weak opening, vague)
- Structure: 55 (no actionable close)
- **Specificity: 35** (no metrics, all vague claims) ← Drives score down
- Pacing: 50 (too brief)
- Presence: 70 (clean delivery)
- **Overall: 53**

### Example 2: Strong Answer (Score: ~82)
**Response:** "I led a pricing optimization project that increased revenue by 23% in Q2. The key insight was segmenting customers by lifetime value, which revealed we were underpricing our enterprise tier. I recommended a phased price increase starting with new customers, then renewals. This generated an additional $1.2M in ARR."

**Grading:**
- Clarity: 85 (clear main point)
- Structure: 80 (good opening, could strengthen close)
- Specificity: 90 (metrics, concrete details, causal reasoning)
- Pacing: 75 (efficient use of time)
- Presence: 80 (confident, minimal filler)
- **Overall: 82**

### Example 3: Executive-Level Answer (Score: ~92)
**Response:** "We reduced customer churn from 8% to 3.2% in six months. Root cause: 70% of churned customers cited slow onboarding. I rebuilt the onboarding workflow, cutting time-to-value from 45 days to 11 days. Result: $4M in retained ARR and NPS increased 22 points. Next step: replicate this playbook across our EMEA segment."

**Grading:**
- Clarity: 95 (immediate, crystal clear)
- Structure: 95 (perfect arc with actionable close)
- Specificity: 95 (multiple metrics, concrete example, impact mapped)
- Pacing: 90 (optimal density)
- Presence: 92 (decisive, zero filler)
- **Overall: 93**

---

This grading system makes Cognify feel **elite**, not **encouraging**.
Users will trust the feedback because it's **tough but fair**.
