# Framework: Behavior → Impact → Expectation (BIE)

A feedback framework for delivering tough-but-kind corrective conversations. Derived from the **SBI** (Situation–Behavior–Impact) model developed at the **Center for Creative Leadership** in the 1990s, extended with an explicit forward-looking Expectation node.

## When to use

- 1:1 performance conversations
- Calling out a specific behavior a teammate exhibited
- Any feedback moment where you need the other person to hear it without getting defensive

## The nodes

1. **Behavior** — the specific observed behavior. Concrete. No interpretation. No character judgment. "In Wednesday's standup you interrupted Priya twice" — not "you're being dismissive lately."
2. **Impact** — the effect that behavior had on the work, the team, or the outcome. Factual, not emotional. "Priya stopped contributing for the rest of the meeting, and we didn't hear the caching concern until after the decision was made."
3. **Expectation** — what you need to happen going forward. Specific, measurable, non-negotiable. "Going forward, I need you to let whoever is speaking finish before you respond, even if you disagree."

## Exemplar phrasing

> "In Wednesday's standup, you interrupted Priya twice while she was explaining the cache issue. [**Behavior**] She stopped contributing for the rest of the meeting, and we didn't hear her point until after the decision was made — which we've now had to reopen. [**Impact**] Going forward, I need you to let whoever is speaking finish before you respond, even when you disagree. [**Expectation**]"

## Common failure modes

- **Generalizing the behavior** — "you interrupt people a lot" instead of "in Wednesday's standup at 9:45". Specificity is what keeps it from feeling like character assassination.
- **Interpreting instead of observing** — "you were being dismissive" is an interpretation; "you cut her off twice" is the behavior. Stay on the observable side.
- **Softening the impact** — "it was kind of awkward" loses the signal. Name the concrete cost.
- **Vague expectation** — "try to be more considerate" is unmeasurable. The Expectation node needs to be a specific, checkable future behavior.
- **Opening with Impact** — "you really hurt Priya's feelings…" puts the listener on the defensive before they know why. Always lead with Behavior.

## Antipatterns

- "I just wanted to say…"
- "This isn't a big deal, but…"
- "You always…" / "You never…"
- Sandwich feedback (praise → criticism → praise) — well-researched as counterproductive; receivers discount the praise and miss the criticism

## Origin

Center for Creative Leadership, Situation-Behavior-Impact (SBI) model, 1990s. Researched at CCL and Harvard. The Expectation extension is widely used in modern management coaching (Kim Scott, *Radical Candor*; Stone & Heen, *Thanks for the Feedback*).
