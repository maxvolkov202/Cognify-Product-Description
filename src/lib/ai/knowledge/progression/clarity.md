# Progression: Clarity

How a coach describes *improvement* in clarity between two reps. Use this to write specific, observable comparisons — never vague ("you got clearer") but always anchored to what changed ("you traded 'basically' for a concrete number").

## What improved clarity sounds like, rep-to-rep

- **Replaced hedges with assertions** — "I think maybe we should" → "we should, because X"
- **Replaced vague nouns with concrete ones** — "the thing" → "the migration ticket"
- **Replaced abstract claims with numbers** — "a lot faster" → "3x faster"
- **Named the main point earlier** — buried the lead at 0:30 last rep, surfaced it at 0:05 this rep
- **Used one sentence per idea** — compound stacking cleared up, each claim standing alone

## What regressed clarity sounds like

- **Added new hedges** — previously direct, now "sort of", "kind of", "I guess"
- **Bigger abstraction gap** — previously named the thing, now back to "stuff" and "things"
- **Longer compound sentences** — idea soup returned
- **Qualifier-stacking** — "I mean, like, basically, sort of" appeared mid-rep

## Coaching observations by delta band

| Delta | What to say |
|---|---|
| +15 or more | Call out the specific switch that drove it. Quote the old hedge and the new assertion side by side. |
| +5 to +14 | One or two specific moments where the phrasing sharpened. Point at the exact phrase. |
| -5 to +4 | Stable. Acknowledge and redirect to the next weakness — clarity isn't the lever right now. |
| -6 or worse | Name the specific regression. Was it hedge creep? Vague nouns? Be precise about what slipped. |

## Still-needs-work signals (even when score improved)

- One or two residual hedges near the close ("sort of", "I think")
- A single vague noun that could be named concretely
- A buried-lead moment — the main point arriving after filler
- Qualifier layering ("just", "really", "actually") stacking on the verb

## Exemplar comparison phrases

- "You cut 'basically' three times in this rep — last rep it appeared six. The core claim now stands alone."
- "Previously the main point arrived at 0:27. This rep it's in the first sentence."
- "You're still hedging right before the close ('I think this is the move') — bring the same directness here that you brought in the opening."
