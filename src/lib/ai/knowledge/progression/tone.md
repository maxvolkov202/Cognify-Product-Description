# Progression: Tone

Tone measures vocal expressiveness — pitch variation, downward inflection on statements, volume contrast, vocal energy, articulation, and warmth. Improvements here are about *the voice doing the work the words can't* — sounding certain when stating a fact, sounding warm when acknowledging a person, holding presence from the first word to the last.

## What improved tone sounds like

- **Pitch range expanded** — wider intentional variation across the response (3+ semitones is the floor for "varied")
- **Statements close downward** — every assertion lands with a falling beat, not a rising question
- **Volume contrast emerged** — important words got louder; transition words got quieter
- **Articulation crisped up** — final consonants land cleanly, no trailing-off into mumbles
- **Energy held to the close** — the last sentence had the same vocal presence as the first
- **Warmth where the content called for it** — acknowledgment moments softened audibly without losing authority

## What regressed tone sounds like

- **Pitch flattened** — variance dropped, multiple seconds of monotone
- **Upspeak crept in** — statements ending with rising pitch, reading as questions
- **Volume locked** — no dynamic range, every word weighted the same
- **Energy faded** — first sentence had presence, last sentence trailed off
- **Mumbled close** — final words dropped or breathy

## Coaching observations by delta band

| Delta | What to say |
|---|---|
| +15 or more | Name the specific vocal moment. Quote the timestamp where the pitch landed or the volume contrast worked. |
| +5 to +14 | One or two moments of vocal variety that improved the read. Point to them with timestamps. |
| -5 to +4 | Stable. Redirect to the next dimension. |
| -6 or worse | Name the specific failure: monotone window, upspeak pattern, or energy fade. Specify *where* in the rep. |

## Still-needs-work signals

- A monotone window of 15+ seconds in the middle of the rep
- Three or more statements in a row ending with rising pitch
- Final sentence delivered noticeably quieter than the average rep volume
- Mumbled or breathy delivery during a key claim

## Exemplar comparison phrases

- "Pitch range jumped from 1.8 semitones last rep to 4.2 this rep. The opening claim — 'here's what changed' — landed because your voice told the listener it was the headline before the words did."
- "Every statement-end pitch went down this rep. Last rep had three rising-pitch closes; that read as uncertainty even though the content was confident."
- "The last sentence held its volume — last rep dropped 8dB on the close. Holding the energy through the final word is what makes a close land."
