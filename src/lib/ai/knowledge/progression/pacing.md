# Progression: Pacing

Pacing is fully deterministic — words-per-minute, pause distribution, and time-budget ratio. Improvements are numerical and objective.

## What improved pacing sounds like

- **WPM moved toward the target band** (≈130–160 WPM for most reps)
- **Long pauses cleared** — no 2+ second dead air outside intentional beats
- **Time-budget ratio near 1.0** — used their time without rushing or trailing off
- **Breath pauses appeared at clause boundaries**, not mid-phrase
- **Consistent tempo** — not starting fast and ending slow (or vice versa)

## What regressed pacing sounds like

- **Rushed** — WPM > 180, clipping consonants
- **Dragged** — WPM < 110, drifting between phrases
- **Runaway end** — tempo spiked in the last 20% to fit the time budget
- **Excess pauses** — multiple 2+ second dead spots that weren't intentional
- **Time-budget wrong** — used 40% of time (too short) or 130% (over-ran)

## Coaching observations by delta band

| Delta | What to say |
|---|---|
| +15 or more | Name the specific number — "WPM went from 175 to 148". Numbers are the whole point here. |
| +5 to +14 | Point at the specific pace shift — opening pace, closing pace, mid-rep settle. |
| -5 to +4 | Stable. Redirect. |
| -6 or worse | Name what happened numerically. Over-ran? Rushed? Dragged? |

## Still-needs-work signals

- Good average WPM but a rushed closer (most common pattern)
- Good average but one 3+ second dead spot mid-rep
- Time-budget fine but the phrasing suggests mental rushing (clipped consonants, swallowed words)

## Exemplar comparison phrases

- "WPM dropped from 178 to 151 — your listener has time to absorb each claim now."
- "Last rep you finished in 22 seconds of a 45-second budget. This rep: 41 seconds. You're using the space."
- "Pace is in the pocket on average, but the last 10 seconds sped back up — the close is rushing."

## Note for the scoring model

Because pacing is deterministic, the score will move with the numbers regardless of semantic judgment. Anchor coaching observations to specific signals in the pacing score's `signals` array.
