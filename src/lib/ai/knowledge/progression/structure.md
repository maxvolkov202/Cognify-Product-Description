# Progression: Structure

How a coach describes *improvement* in structure between two reps. Structure improvements are typically visible — you can point at the opening, the connectors, the close.

## What improved structure sounds like

- **Named the frame in the first 10s** — "three things about the migration" instead of a cold start
- **Explicit connectors appeared** — "first", "because", "which brings us to", "the second reason"
- **Closing restates the opening** — circular landing, listener knows the rep is over
- **Consistent ordering** — picked chronological OR causal OR importance and held it
- **Through-line held** — one main idea, not three competing claims

## What regressed structure sounds like

- **Lost the opening** — back to "so…" cold starts
- **Connectors disappeared** — ideas adjacent with no bridge
- **Topic jumping** — migrated mid-rep from one subject to another without signposting
- **Trailing close** — "so yeah, that's about it" instead of landing the point
- **Missing through-line** — three mini-reps stitched together

## Coaching observations by delta band

| Delta | What to say |
|---|---|
| +15 or more | Name the structural scaffold they used this time. Was it PREP? Three-act? Call the shape. |
| +5 to +14 | Point at the specific transition or opening that sharpened. One specific moment. |
| -5 to +4 | Stable. Structure isn't the biggest lever in this rep — redirect. |
| -6 or worse | Name what disappeared: was it the opening, the connectors, or the close? Be specific. |

## Still-needs-work signals (even when score improved)

- Opening is there but could be crisper (lead-in phrases before the actual hook)
- One transition missing between otherwise-structured sections
- Close lands but trails into tangent ("oh, and one more thing…")
- Frame was stated but not reinforced — "three things" promised, listener isn't sure which was the third

## Exemplar comparison phrases

- "Last rep opened with 'so, um, with the migration'. This rep opened with 'three things about the migration' — the listener had a count in the first four seconds."
- "You introduced 'first' and 'second' as connectors this rep. Next: add the third one explicitly — right now the third point sneaks in."
- "The close in this rep ('that's why we should ship Monday') ties back to the opening. Previously the rep ended on a tangent."
