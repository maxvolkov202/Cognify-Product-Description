# Progression: Confidence

Confidence is a hybrid score (deterministic hedges/restarts + LLM semantic). Improvements are visible in both the word count of hedges AND the assertiveness of the claims.

## What improved confidence sounds like

- **Hedges dropped** — "sort of", "kind of", "I think", "I guess", "just", "maybe" reduced in count
- **Restarts dropped** — fewer self-interruptions and re-phrasings
- **Declarative sentences** — "we should" instead of "I think we might want to consider"
- **Ownership of the claim** — "I recommend" instead of "people are saying"
- **No apologetic preamble** — no "so, um, I don't know if this is right but…"

## What regressed confidence sounds like

- **Hedge creep** — layered qualifiers appeared on previously clean claims
- **Apologetic framing returned** — "just wanted to", "maybe this is wrong"
- **Upward inflection on declaratives** — (hard to score without audio, but note if the LLM flagged it)
- **Longer hedge chains** — "I mean, like, sort of, I guess" stacking

## Coaching observations by delta band

| Delta | What to say |
|---|---|
| +15 or more | Quote the specific assertion that landed with no hedging. Contrast with the hedged version from last rep. |
| +5 to +14 | One or two specific hedges dropped. Call them out by count or by phrase. |
| -5 to +4 | Stable. Redirect. |
| -6 or worse | Name the specific hedge that returned. Was it "I think"? "Sort of"? Be precise. |

## Still-needs-work signals

- One residual "I think" near the strongest claim in the rep
- An apology baked into the open ("sorry if this is scattered")
- A restart right before the landing phrase
- "Just" on the main verb ("I just wanted to say")

## Exemplar comparison phrases

- "Last rep you said 'I think maybe we should consider'. This rep: 'we should'. Same content, zero hedge."
- "You dropped three 'sort of's between reps — deterministic signal. That's real."
- "The core claim is direct, but the close ('I hope that makes sense?') drops the confidence you built. Land it flat."
