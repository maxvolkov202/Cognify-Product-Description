# Domain: Cold Calling

Cold calling is B2B sales outreach where the caller has no prior relationship with the prospect. It stress-tests every communication dimension under maximum social friction: the listener did not ask for this call and is primed to end it.

## Why this matters for Cognify

Cold calls compress an entire communication challenge into 30–45 seconds. Every word has to earn the next word. A failed opener loses the call; a rambling value statement loses the meeting. Training on cold-call scenarios forces clarity, structure, conciseness, and handling-pressure into the same rep. First-tier domain for Daily Workout and Scenario Training prompt banks.

## Current practitioners (2025–2026)

### Connor Murray & Eric Finch — Higher Levels
*Connor: Enterprise AE at Datadog, ex-Oracle #1 SDR / SDR manager. Eric: strategic AE. Higher Levels' "Cold Call Mastery" is the modern tech-sales gold standard.*

**The 3-part framework — total under 35 seconds.**

1. **Opener** — assumptive formality with downward inflection.
   > "Hey Eric, this is Connor Murray calling from [Company]. How are you?"

2. **Value statement** — who you are, why you're calling, what you want.
   > "I'm part of the team that supports [industry] [role] on [domain]. We help with [priorities / pain points], typically by [outcome or transformation]."
   > "I know I called you out of the blue here — just looking to set aside a time next week to get introduced and align with your priorities."

3. **Close** — two specific time options.
   > "How's your calendar next week on Wednesday or Thursday?"

**Objection handling — the "double-down" pattern.** Acknowledge, then re-sell the meeting instead of disqualifying.
> "Totally get that. That's actually why I'm reaching out. We work with customers of [Competitor] all the time. This is more to get introduced and align on your priorities going forward. Would Wednesday or Thursday work?"

**Key principles:**
- Ask twice before pivoting to follow-up questions
- Prepare 3–5 objection flashcards and memorize responses
- Send the agenda invite live, mid-call, and confirm delivery: "I just sent the invite — mind checking that it went through?"

### Armand Farrokh & Nick Cegelski — 30 Minutes to President's Club
*Co-hosts of the 30MPC podcast, authors of* Cold Calling Sucks (And That's Why It Works) *(2024). Armand: VP Sales at 29, ex-Pave, ex-Carta. Nick: 3× top enterprise seller.*

**Signature moves:**
- **"Heard the name tossed around"** opener — leads with context/familiarity instead of permission begging
- **Problem proposition** — lead with a vivid, emotionally-charged problem the buyer actually feels, *then* briefly show how you solve it
- **"Speak in dumb human language"** — kill marketing jargon, talk like a real person
- **Mr. Miyagi objection handling** — absorb the objection instead of fighting it

### Chris Orlob — pclub.io

## Gong Labs data — 2025 findings

- Asking **11–14 questions** per call correlates with **70%+** cold-call success
- Persistence: **39%** connect rate on first attempt → **93%** by the third
- Stating the reason for your call makes cold calls **2.1×** more successful
- Asking how the buyer has been performs **6.6×** better
- Talk-to-listen ratio on successful cold calls: **55:45** (cold calls are different from discovery — you're there to educate, not ask)
- **"Did I catch you at a bad time?"** makes you **40% less likely** to book a meeting
- Average rep sends **344 cold emails** per meeting booked (phone compounds this)

## Common failure modes

- **Permission begging** — "Is now a bad time?" / "Do you have a quick minute?"
- **Marketing jargon** — "best-of-breed end-to-end AI-powered solution"
- **Leading with the product** instead of the problem
- **Pitch longer than 45 seconds** — prospect has already decided
- **Pivoting to discovery too early** — the prospect is not on a discovery call yet
- **Disqualifying at first objection** — objection ≠ rejection
- **Vague close** — "maybe sometime next week?"

## Exemplar phrasings (what high-scoring reps sound like)

- **Strong opener**: "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. Quick reason for the call — I noticed [specific trigger], and I help [role] with [specific problem]. Worth a quick intro Tuesday or Thursday?"
- **Problem proposition**: "Most [role] I talk to lose an hour a day on [specific pain]. We cut that to zero."
- **Double-down objection response**: "Totally get it. That's actually why I called — [specific reason that reframes the objection]."
- **Specific close**: "Wednesday at 2 or Thursday at 10?"

## Antipatterns to flag in scoring

- "Hi, I know you're busy so I'll be quick…"
- "I was wondering if I could take 15 minutes of your time to introduce our best-in-class platform…"
- "We're a cloud-based end-to-end AI-powered…"
- "Do you have a moment?"
- "Is this a bad time?"
