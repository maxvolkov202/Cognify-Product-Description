# Skill: Clarity

## Definition
Ideas land on first hearing. No re-interpretation needed. Concrete language, audience-appropriate vocabulary, resolved pronouns, main point stated early.

## HIGH clarity — recognize these patterns
- Main point in the first 10 seconds (BLUF / Answer-first)
- Concrete nouns: "the checkout flow", "the Tuesday deploy", "the validation endpoint"
- Every pronoun (it/they/that) has a clear antecedent within 2 sentences
- Vocabulary matches the audience register
- No throat-clearing preambles — sentence one carries signal

## LOW clarity — recognize these patterns
- Unresolved pronouns ("and *it* kind of became a problem because *they* wanted *that*…")
- Abstract noun dependency ("the system", "the process", "the situation", "the thing")
- Jargon mismatched to audience (engineering jargon to a CFO, exec-speak to an engineer)
- Listener-pauses-to-re-interpret moments mid-sentence
- Main point doesn't land until the final 10 seconds (or never)

## Banned-as-clarity-killer antipatterns (flag in callouts)
- "Basically…" as opener
- "The thing is…"
- "What I mean by that is…" / "Let me explain what I mean…"
- "You know what I mean?"
- "So yeah…" as opener
- Strings of >3 pronouns without intervening concrete nouns

## Edge-case rules (override per-dim rubric when in conflict)
- Brevity that *erases* meaning takes the hit on Clarity, not as Conciseness reward
- Shallow-but-organized: structure can be high while clarity stays low if ideas don't land
- Short-but-deep: <30s response that fully resolves the prompt CAN score high on clarity
- Audience-mismatch is the single most common low-clarity failure mode

## Quote-driven callout shape
- positive: quote a verbatim concrete moment, name the specificity
- warn/critical: quote the unresolved pronoun / abstract noun moment, suggest a concrete rewrite
