# Skill: Structure

## Definition
Visible scaffolding the listener can follow without effort. Opening that establishes direction, logical connectors between points, close that lands the main point. Listener can reconstruct the argument afterward.

## HIGH structure — recognize these patterns
- Opening within 10s that signals direction ("Three things about the migration. First…")
- Explicit connectors: "first/second/third", "because", "therefore", "which brings us to"
- Consistent ordering — chronological OR causal OR importance (one, not mixed)
- Close that reinforces the main point or names the action
- Listener could outline the rep in 3 lines after one hearing

## LOW structure — recognize these patterns
- No visible opening — starts mid-thought ("so with the migration…")
- No visible close — trails off ("so… yeah, that's what's going on")
- Topic jumps with no bridge — one idea ends, another begins, no connector
- Random ordering — ideas in the sequence they occurred to the speaker
- Multiple main points competing — no through-line

## Banned-as-structure-killer antipatterns (flag in callouts)
- "Anyways…" as transition
- "And then…" as the only connector
- "So…" as opener
- "So… yeah" as closer
- "One more thing…" tacked onto the end
- Listing without ordering ("a few thoughts: X, Y, Z…" with no logic)

## Edge-case rules (override per-dim rubric when in conflict)
- Shallow-but-organized: structure can score HIGH while thinking_quality stays LOW. Do not let strong scaffolding compensate for weak reasoning.
- Framework rep (scenario mode): also score `structural_adherence` against the framework's nodes in order — separate from this dim.
- Short-but-deep: a 25s response that fully resolved the prompt with clear arc can score 85+ on structure.

## Quote-driven callout shape
- positive: quote the opener or close that landed; name the connector or arc
- warn/critical: quote the missing-transition moment, suggest a one-word connector to add
